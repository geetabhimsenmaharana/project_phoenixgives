Saturday by Saturday

Stories from the giving.

Each entry below is a real Saturday — a small offering, a note written by hand, a moment shared with someone whose name I may never know. These are placeholder stories for now; swap in your own as each Saturday happens.

Saturday — Week 1

The sweater I almost kept

It was a soft grey sweater — one I still loved, honestly. I wrapped it in last Sunday's newspaper and wrote across the front in pencil: "This is yours. Take it freely. You deserve this." I left it folded on a bench near the bus stop and walked away without looking back. That not-looking-back is the hardest, and the most important, part.

Giving without watching for a reaction is what seva is supposed to feel like — offered, not performed.

Saturday — Week 2

A quote for a stranger

This Saturday I tore a page from the classifieds and wrote one of my favorite lines across it, signed "by PhoenixGives." Underneath, I tucked a small bag of clothes still in good shape — shirts I'd outgrown in style if not in size. No name attached, no way to trace it back to me. Just a small reminder that someone, somewhere, thought of them.

Saturday — Week 3

Beyond clothes — books and a pen

PhoenixGives started with clothes, but this Saturday I added two books I'd already read twice and a pen that still writes beautifully. I wrapped them together with the same note I always come back to: "Sending you warmth and hope today." The plan going forward is to keep expanding — fresh food some weeks, small useful things other weeks — whatever I can genuinely spare.

"We recognize that every person we serve is a reflection of the same presence, moving through different circumstances."

— PhoenixGives
